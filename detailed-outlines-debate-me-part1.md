# DETAILED CHAPTER OUTLINES: Debate Me

## Chapter 1: The Championship Mindset
**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words  
**POV:** Jordan Hayes, first person  
**Setting:** Wellington Hall debate practice room, Connecticut  
**Time:** Late afternoon, early April

**Purpose:** Establish Jordan as fierce debate captain determined to win nationals and defeat David Sterling.

**Beats:**
- Jordan runs Wellington debate team practice with intensity, drilling members on argumentation technique and rebuttal speed. Her scholarship student background drives her need to prove herself through excellence.
- Riley Morrison mentions Oakridge Prep's strong preparation; Jordan's reaction reveals two-year rivalry with David Sterling, their legendary competitions defining New England debate circuit.
- Alone after practice, Jordan reviews video footage of last tournament where David beat her by three points, analyzing every moment, identifying where she lost ground.
- Jordan makes internal promise: this year at nationals she will finally defeat David Sterling and prove she's the best debater in the region, validating her place at Wellington.

**Dialogue Topics:** Jordan directing practice drills, correcting technique, pushing team harder. Riley cautioning about burnout, mentioning Oakridge competition. Jordan dismissing concerns, focused only on winning strategy.

**Emotional Arc:** Jordan begins confident and driven but shows underlying insecurity about being scholarship student at elite school. Her determination masks fear of not being good enough.

**Key Details:** Debate practice room with championship plaques, worn tables, whiteboard filled with argument structures. Jordan's meticulous notes, video analysis setup. Wellington blazer, debate case materials. References to tournament circuit, point spreads, judging criteria.

**Key Details:** Dorm room with debate trophies, laptop showing tournament websites, photos of past competitions. Wellington hoodie, textbooks scattered around. References to debate circuit gossip, Sterling family reputation, tournament rankings.

**Key Details:** Coach's office with filing cabinets, tournament schedules, budget spreadsheets. Debate room with team gathered around table, financial calculations on whiteboard. Empty practice room at dusk, Jordan alone with trophy case visible. References to exact costs, school administration, endowment issues.

**Emotional Arc:** Jordan cycles through denial, anger, bargaining before reaching reluctant acceptance. Her desperation to compete outweighs her hatred of the arrangement, showing how much nationals means to her.

**Key Details:** Coach's office phone call, Jordan pacing her dorm room during conversation. Dorm room with debate materials everywhere, laptop showing tournament info. Text messages with Riley. References to twelve-passenger van, two-day drive, hotel arrangements, debate consortium coordination.

Van interior with four rows, coaches in front, captains in back. Highway entrance, beginning of six-hundred-mile journey. I-95 South signage.

**Key Details:** Highway rest stop with multiple fast food options, convenience store, crowded restrooms. Food court seating area where teams regroup. Van parked in lot. Shared snack purchase of chips and drinks becoming point of contention. Other travelers watching the argument.
